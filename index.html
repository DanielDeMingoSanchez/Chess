Pieces of chess

King (K):
Each player has one king. The king is the most important piece but is not very mobile. If a player's king is under direct threat of capture (check), the player must make a move to remove the threat.
Queen (Q):
Each player starts with one queen. The queen is the most powerful piece, capable of moving any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
Rook (R):
Each player has two rooks. Rooks move horizontally or vertically any number of squares.
Knight (N):
Each player has two knights. Knights move in an "L" shape: two squares in one direction (horizontally or vertically) and then one square perpendicular to that.
Bishop (B):
Each player has two bishops. Bishops move diagonally any number of squares.
Pawn (P):
Each player has eight pawns. Pawns move forward but capture diagonally. On their first move, pawns have the option to move forward two squares.
